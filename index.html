Monte Carlo Methods and Random Sampling

An overview of Monte Carlo methods with examples of frequently used techniques
Seminar Paper
Monte Carlo methods provide essential computational tools for solving high-dimensional integration and sampling problems that are analytically intractable. This paper presents a comprehensive treatment of Monte Carlo techniques, beginning with fundamental integration principles and the curse of dimensionality that motivates probabilistic approaches. Key sampling algorithms are systematically developed, including the inversion method, Box-Muller transformation, and acceptance-rejection techniques. The paper presents variance reduction strategies, most importantly importance sampling, and complements pseudo-random approaches with quasi-Monte Carlo methods for improved convergence rates. Practical effectiveness is demonstrated through classical applications including Ï€ approximation and rare event estimation in financial risk models, where importance sampling achieves dramatic efficiency gains over standard approaches.
